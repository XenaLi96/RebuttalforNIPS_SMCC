\textbf{Opening response.}
We thank the reviewer for the careful and constructive assessment. We agree with the
three central requests: quantify uncertainty in the Visium HD cell construction,
demonstrate breadth beyond a few case studies, and calibrate low correlations with
biologically interpretable and simple baselines. We also agree that in-domain and
cross-patient results answer different questions and should not be conflated.

\medskip
\textbf{vCPF-1.}\quad
\revcomment{How reliable is Visium HD bin-to-cell aggregation? How are boundary bins
assigned, and do segmentation errors affect the resulting profiles?}

\response
We will clarify the claim boundary first. Xenium supplies natively cell-resolved
transcript locations and platform-defined cell masks. Visium HD instead supplies
dense 8-\(\mu\)m bins; we register these bins to the H\&E image, segment cell
footprints, and aggregate bin counts to the aligned footprints. Thus, Visium HD
records are \emph{cell-aligned computational targets}, not direct single-cell
measurements. We will replace wording that could imply otherwise.

\needinfo{State the exact boundary-bin rule used in the released pipeline
(intersection threshold, tie handling, treatment of bins overlapping multiple cells,
and discarded-bin criteria), together with the segmentation model/version and all QC
thresholds.}

\needexp{Report a boundary/segmentation sensitivity analysis: strict boundary-bin
discard versus the released assignment rule; mask erosion and dilation; fraction of
cells/bins retained; expression-profile agreement; marker consistency; and
downstream prediction changes with confidence intervals. Where matched Xenium or
another cell-resolved reference is available, add an external concordance check.}

These results will be reported separately for Visium HD and Xenium, because
combining them would obscure the uncertainty specific to computational aggregation.

\medskip
\textbf{vCPF-3.}\quad
\revcomment{How should low gene-wise correlations, especially under transfer, be
interpreted biologically? Please add marker/cell-type-gene or spatial-only/metadata-only
baselines.}

\response
We agree that the low full-gene correlations are important negative results, not
numbers to hide. Full-gene evaluation contains many sparse and weakly predictable
genes; cross-patient shifts further change morphology, tissue composition, and
acquisition conditions. For the lung experiment, STBoost-Ref obtains gene-wise
Pearson/Spearman and cell-wise Pearson correlations of
\(0.0259/0.0268/0.4112\) in-domain and
\(0.0124/0.0137/0.2820\) cross-patient over the full panel. These values indicate
that histology alone is not a reliable replacement for measured expression.

As a diagnostic rather than a replacement for full-gene evaluation, we also
examined 50 genes selected within each regime. STBoost-Ref reaches
\(0.5117/0.5069/0.5979\) in-domain and
\(0.2986/0.2405/0.3451\) cross-patient (gene Pearson/gene Spearman/cell Pearson).
Because this subset was selected post hoc by STBoost-Ref performance, we will label
it explicitly as an upper-bound diagnostic and not as an unbiased marker-gene
benchmark.

For biological calibration, inferred expression from 111,772 lung cells supports
coarse marker-derived cell-state prediction with \(0.5935\) accuracy and \(0.5922\)
balanced accuracy (ARI \(0.1362\), NMI \(0.2154\)). This shows partial recovery of
cell-state signal while the modest clustering agreement cautions against
cell-by-cell biological interpretation.

\needexp{Add a prespecified marker-gene/cell-type-associated-gene evaluation with no
test-set gene selection, including marker recovery, direction agreement, and
cell-state performance from predicted versus measured expression.}

\needexp{Add spatial-only and metadata-only baselines, using only training-split
information at query time, and report them under the same in-domain and
cross-patient splits.}

\medskip
\textbf{vCPF-4.}\quad
\revcomment{In-domain splits may measure local interpolation and spatial
autocorrelation rather than robust generalization.}

\response
We agree. We will relabel the in-domain setting as an interpolation diagnostic and
use cross-patient/cross-study transfer for the main generalization claim. In the lung
experiment, the drop from \(0.4112\) to \(0.2820\) in full-panel cell-wise Pearson
correlation illustrates this gap.

\needexp{Add a distance-buffered in-domain split and a coordinate-only/spatial-neighbor
baseline, reporting performance as a function of the minimum train--test distance.}

\medskip
\textbf{vCPF-5.}\quad
\revcomment{The ovarian age-shift experiment is narrow and does not establish that
all context variables can be evaluated systematically.}

\response
We agree and will describe it as one case study, not as proof of systematic context
evaluation. Moreover, the current comparisons differ in patient/tumour context and
gene-panel size, so they cannot identify a pure age effect. We will define AK, AD,
and AL as anonymized ovarian sample identifiers and state their ages and other known
covariates explicitly.

\needexp{Rerun the ovarian comparison on the identical shared gene panel and matched
preprocessing, report sample/cell counts and uncertainty, and rewrite the conclusion
as an age-associated shift only if the result remains supported after acknowledging
patient and tissue-state confounding.}
